Meghan Newell is a art director, designer, illustrator and animator with extensive experience spanning ad agencies, design studios, and in-house teams.

Recently, I've spoken, experimented, and engaged with augmented reality as an Immersive Designer at Google and a resident in Mozilla's XR Studio program, in the process designing, developing, and pitching my own AR app. I then found a home as Brand Design Lead at Pocket.

As a senior creative at Lyft, I enjoyed building the brand for five years as the company scaled from a tiny start-up of 70 to a nationally known company. I created everything from explainers and training videos, to brand guidelines and email templates, to landing pages and social content, as well as printed schwag, booklets and brochures. I art directed campaigns and outside agencies, managed designers, and made endless ads, as well as contributing to the design of our app.

Previously, I spent about a decade freelancing for ad agencies, design studios and direct-to-client doing motion/interactive art direction, design, illustration and animation - highlights include design work on the groundbreaking apple ipod silhouette spots, designing tv titles and show packages for MTV, VH1 and Current TV, storyboard design for many national commercials, and design work on high profile websites for blockbuster movies and fancy automobiles.

I love everything involved with visual communication, and thrive on using keen observation, curiosity, and a dual obsession with technology and design to create beautiful solutions.
